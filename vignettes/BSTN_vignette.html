Skewed tensor response model

For an order-KK tensor response Yi∈Rd1×⋯×dKYi∈Rd1×⋯×dK with a vector of covariates xi∈Rpxi∈Rp, we consider a tensor response regression model

Yi=B×¯(K+1)xi+Ei,  for  i=1,…,n, Yi=B×¯(K+1)xi+Ei,  for  i=1,…,n, 
where B∈Rd1×⋯×dK×pB∈Rd1×⋯×dK×p is an order-(K+1)(K+1) unknown tensor of regression coefficients, ×¯(K+1)×¯(K+1) is the (K+1)(K+1)-mode vector product, and the error Ei∈Rd1×⋯×dKEi∈Rd1×⋯×dK is an order-KK tensor. We model the skewness in the distribution of YY via
Ei=|Z2i|×KΛΛ+Z1i,Ei=|Z2i|×KΛΛ+Z1i,
 where ΛΛ=diag(λ1,…,λdK)∈RdK×dKΛΛ=diag(λ1,…,λdK)∈RdK×dK with skewness parameters λλ=(λ1,…,λdK)λλ=(λ1,…,λdK) and |M||M| denotes the matrix whose each element of is the absolute value of the corresponding element of matrix MM.

Tensor Spike-and-Slab prior

Bi1i2i3j=ηi3j+γi1i2i3j,  ηi3j∼N(0,τ2)  for  τ>0Bi1i2i3j=ηi3j+γi1i2i3j,  ηi3j∼N(0,τ2)  for  τ>0
and introduce sparsity via spike-and-slab priors as
γi1i2i3j∼iid(1−ωi1i2i3j)δ0(γi1i2i3j)+ωi1i2i3jN(0,ν2)  for  ν>0,γi1i2i3j∼iid(1−ωi1i2i3j)δ0(γi1i2i3j)+ωi1i2i3jN(0,ν2)  for  ν>0,
 where, δ0(⋅)δ0(⋅) is the indicator function at 0 and ωi1i2i3j∼iidBernoulli(ψ)ωi1i2i3j∼iidBernoulli(ψ) with hyperprior ψ∼Beta(a,b)ψ∼Beta(a,b). For the GAAD study, we choose a=b=0.1a=b=0.1, representing a vague hyperprior for ψψ.

Since sparsity inducing prior on γi1i2i3jγi1i2i3j tends to concentrate around zero, BB in is mostly determined by ηi3jηi3j allowing parsimonious interpretation of covariate effects, while estimates of BB using Tensor Normal prior (with large-scale covariances) are much more different from each other.

D-Statistic

D=d1/|d2|D=d1/|d2| for each regression effects Bi1i2i3jBi1i2i3j, where d1d1 is distance of the Bayesian estimate (posterior median) from null value (0 here) and d2d2 is the distance of the Bayes estimate from the nearest endpoint of the 95% CI of the parameter. Here, D>1.5D>1.5 (D<−1.5)(D<−1.5) implies strong posterior evidence of positive (negative) effect of covariate xjxj on the biomarker i3i3 at site i2i2 of tooth i1i1. Similarly, the range −0.8<D<0.8−0.8<D<0.8 suggests moderate evidence for null hypothesis, and 0.8≤D≤1.50.8≤D≤1.5 represents moderate positive effect, and −1.5≤D≤−0.8−1.5≤D≤−0.8 denotes moderate negative effect of xjxj on Yi1i2i3Yi1i2i3.
